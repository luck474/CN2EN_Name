# Style Directions

Use this reference only when the user wants tighter stylistic iteration.

## Literary

Aim for names that feel composed, thoughtful, and quietly expressive.

Common traits:
- Soft but clear consonants
- A sense of depth without sounding antique
- Familiar enough to use daily

Examples of the direction:
- Rowan
- Auden
- Ellis
- Soren
- Arden

## Modern

Aim for names that feel concise, polished, and current.

Common traits:
- Short to medium length
- Clean spelling
- Internationally easy to read

Examples of the direction:
- Evan
- Ari
- Ellis
- Rowan
- Milan

## Scholarly

Aim for names with restraint, gravity, and intellectual texture.

Common traits:
- Slight austerity
- Understated authority
- A sense of taste rather than trend

Examples of the direction:
- Auden
- Ellis
- Morgan
- Soren
- Emrys

## Design-Forward Neutral

Aim for names that feel curated, elegant, and visually clean.

Common traits:
- Strong silhouette on the page
- Balanced sound
- Memorable without excess

Examples of the direction:
- Ari
- Rowan
- Ellis
- Luca
- Remy

## Matching a Chinese Name

When adapting from a Chinese name, prioritize one of these:

1. Sound echo
- Preserve a syllable or rhythm if it still sounds natural in English.

2. Mood echo
- Preserve the emotional texture, such as calm, bright, refined, or expansive.

3. Image echo
- Preserve an associated image, such as summer, light, elegance, or simplicity.

If multiple paths are possible, offer one option from each path and explain the tradeoff.
